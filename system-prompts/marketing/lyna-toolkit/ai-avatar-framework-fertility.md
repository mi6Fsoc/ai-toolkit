# AI Avatar Framework: Women's Nutrition Influencer
### Fertility Persona — Base Prompt System

> **How to use this framework:**
> The **Character Creation Prompt** locks the avatar's identity, appearance, and personality — generate this once and treat it as the source of truth.
> The **Character Sheet Prompt** generates the multi-angle reference board interns use when creating scene/video content.
> Interns fill in only `[SCENE]`, `[HOOK]`, `[TALKING POINTS]`, and `[CTA]` when producing content. Everything else stays locked.

## PART 1 — CHARACTER CREATION PROMPT

---

### Full Version

---

**PROMPT:**
Create a photorealistic AI avatar for a [PLATFORM: e.g. Instagram / TikTok / YouTube Shorts] content series targeting women experiencing fertility challenges. This avatar is a trusted female health educator and nutrition expert. She must feel like a credible but warm real-person creator — not a generic influencer, not a clinical figure. She is the woman your audience wishes they had as a knowledgeable friend.

---

**CORE DIRECTIVE (NON-NEGOTIABLE):**
This avatar must be indistinguishable from a real human creator. No AI-typical smoothness, symmetry, or perfection. She must carry subtle realism: natural skin texture, expressive eyes, minor asymmetry, lived-in warmth. Her face must be memorably distinct — not generic "attractive woman." Every design choice reinforces trust, warmth, and credibility. This character must remain IDENTICAL across all future content generations.

---

**CHARACTER IDENTITY:**
- Name: [Full name — e.g. first and last]
- Age: [Apparent age, e.g. early 30s]
- Nationality / Ethnicity: [e.g. Western European, American, mixed heritage]
- Design Language: [e.g. Warm cinematic realism — not anime, not stylized]
- Audience Mirror: [Does she look like someone the target audience (women 25–42, fertility journey) would trust and relate to?]
- Platform Energy: [e.g. Instagram educator / TikTok explainer / YouTube long-form host]

---

**FACE DESIGN:**
- Face Shape: [e.g. Soft oval, natural bone structure — approachable, not editorial]
- Skin: [Tone, texture — must include natural imperfections: slight pores, subtle under-eye softness, no airbrushed look]
- Eyes: [Color, size, expression style — should convey warmth and intelligence simultaneously]
- Hair: [Style, color, texture, movement — natural, not over-styled]
- Distinct Features: [One or two memorable traits: e.g. subtle freckles, warm smile lines, defined brows]
- Makeup: [Minimal to moderate — clean skin, soft eye definition, no heavy contouring]

---

**PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE:**
- Core Traits: [3–5 traits — e.g. empathetic, science-literate, calm, non-judgmental, quietly confident]
- Communication Style: [How she speaks — e.g. warm and direct, accessible science, never condescending]
- Emotional Signature: [What feeling she leaves viewers with — e.g. "I feel seen and hopeful"]
- Audience Relationship: [e.g. trusted friend with expertise — not a doctor, not a guru]
- Content Tone: [e.g. validation-first → explanation → actionable hope]

---

**PROFESSIONAL BACKGROUND (VISUAL CUES ONLY):**
- Role: [e.g. Women's nutrition specialist / Fertility health educator]
- Visual Authority Cues: [e.g. minimal lab-adjacent styling, clean clinical backgrounds, structured but casual clothing]
- Props: [e.g. supplement bottles, food items, tablet/phone with data, journal — items that reinforce her expertise organically]
- Environment: [e.g. clean modern kitchen, minimal home studio, bright natural light setting]

---

**PERFORMANCE DIRECTION:**
Avatar must feel mid-thought, not rehearsed. Expressions should reflect the emotional weight of fertility content: calm empathy, measured excitement when sharing a breakthrough insight, genuine care.
- Eye contact: [Direct to camera — builds parasocial trust]
- Expression baseline: [e.g. warm neutral — slight open smile, attentive brow]
- Micro-expressions to capture: [e.g. empathetic nod, thoughtful pause, knowing look]
- Energy register: [e.g. calm and grounded — never frantic, never flat]
- Body language: [e.g. slight forward lean when explaining, open posture, no crossed arms]

---

**WARDROBE:**
- Palette: [e.g. soft neutrals, warm whites, sage greens, dusty rose — aligned with women's wellness aesthetic]
- Garment 1: [Primary outfit — fabric, fit, feel]
- Garment 2: [Alt outfit for variety — different tone or context]
- Layering: [e.g. open shirt over simple tee, soft knit layering]
- Jewelry: [Minimal and personal — e.g. thin gold chain, small studs]
- No logos. No branded items unless specified.

---

**MATERIAL ACCURACY:**
Fabrics must show natural drape, subtle creasing, realistic weight. No plastic or synthetic-looking textures. Skin must interact naturally with light sources. Hair must show individual strand detail and natural volume variation. Avoid the "rendered" look at all costs.

---

**OUTPUT:**
Photorealistic. Suitable for use as a static reference image, profile image, and anchor for all future video/scene content generations. Lighting: soft, natural, warm. Background: clean and neutral. Expression: warm neutral — open and approachable. This is the MASTER REFERENCE image. All future generations must match it exactly.

## PART 2 — CHARACTER SHEET PROMPT

*Use this to generate the multi-angle reference board. Share with all interns before any content production begins.*

---

### Full Version

---

**PROMPT:**
Create a production-grade AI avatar character sheet for a women's health content series. Avatar name: [AVATAR NAME]. This sheet is the master visual reference used by a content team to ensure perfect consistency across all generated scenes, videos, and ad creatives.

---

**CORE DIRECTIVE (NON-NEGOTIABLE):**
No generic layouts. No evenly spaced grids. Composition must feel art-directed and intentional. Every view must match the master reference image with zero drift in face shape, skin tone, hair, or wardrobe. This is a working production asset, not a model sheet.

---

**TURNAROUND (STRICT):**
Full-body front | 3/4 front | Side profile | 3/4 back | Full back.
Identical proportions, skin tone, hair, and wardrobe across every angle. No reinterpretation. No drift.

---

**HEAD STUDY:**
Front (neutral warm) | 3/4 (primary on-camera persona) | Profile (structure reference) | Looking down (empathetic listening) | Looking up (thoughtful) | Dynamic angle (mid-explanation — eyes lit, slight lean forward).
All expressions mid-thought, not posed.

---

**CINEMATIC PORTRAIT:**
- Environment: [e.g. bright modern kitchen / minimal home studio]
- Lighting: Soft natural key light — warm fill, gentle rim separation
- Color Tone: [e.g. warm neutral / soft daylight]
- Expression: [Specific moment — e.g. "about to share a breakthrough insight"]
- Camera: 85mm equivalent, shallow depth of field, cinematic warmth

---

**LAYOUT:**
Clean art-directed sheet. Warm off-white or light neutral background. Include: height indicator, annotation callouts for key facial features, wardrobe notes, color palette swatches, and a production note field for intern guidance.

---

**STYLE:**
Photorealistic cinematic — warm, grounded, trustworthy. No stylized or illustrated look. Must include: natural skin rendering, soft cinematic lighting, high emotional readability, human imperfection.

---

**CONSISTENCY RULE (STRICT):**
Face, proportions, skin tone, hair color and texture, wardrobe, and all distinguishing features must remain IDENTICAL across all views and all future content generations. No reinterpretation between angles. Ever. Interns must use this sheet as the sole visual reference.

---

**OUTPUT:**
Extremely high detail. Sharp focus. Production-ready. Suitable for use as a brief document for Google Flow / Veo / NanoBanana scene generation and ad creative production.

## INTERN USAGE GUIDE

> Once the Character Sheet is approved, interns use the following variables only. Everything else is locked.

```
AVATAR: Nora Ellis [attach Character Sheet reference]

SCENE: [Where is she? e.g. kitchen, studio, outdoors]
LIGHTING: [Natural day / warm evening / soft studio]
HOOK: [Opening line or visual hook — e.g. "If your cycle is irregular, your body is trying to tell you something."]
TALKING POINTS: [Bullet list of what she explains in this video]
CTA: [e.g. "Download the app to track your cycle and nutrition in one place."]
EMOTION: [e.g. empathetic and calm / slightly urgent / hopeful]
WARDROBE: [Sage top / White linen / specify if variation needed]
```

> ⚠️ Do NOT modify face, body proportions, hair, or distinguishing features.
> ⚠️ Always attach the Character Sheet PDF to every generation brief.
> ⚠️ If output drifts from reference, regenerate — do not use.
