## Target Format

**Duration:** 30–45 seconds of spoken content (approximately 80–120 words).
**Platform:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts.
**Style:** Talking head, direct to camera, UGC-adjacent.

This is not a brand explainer. It is not an ad. It is a short education piece that
happens to end with an app download suggestion. The content earns the CTA.

---

## What Makes This Style Work

Three psychological mechanics drive retention and conversion:

1. **Immediate identification.** The opening line makes the viewer think "that's
   me." It names a specific symptom or frustration — not a broad topic.

2. **Sensory anchoring.** One concrete physical detail makes the problem feel
   real and remembered. This is the detail that triggers pause and share behaviour.

3. **Reframe + relief.** A simple, surprising cause explanation makes the viewer
   feel smart for finally understanding their body. The solution is the payoff.
   The close seals it with hope, urgency, or relief.

Pacing is fast but never rushed. Every sentence earns its place. For 30–45s, there
is no room for filler. Each beat must carry weight.

---

## Script Structure (5 Beats — Non-Negotiable)

```
Beat 1 · HOOK (0–3s)      Sharp symptom or frustration. One sentence. Personal.
Beat 2 · SENSORY (3–8s)   One visual or bodily detail that makes it feel real.
Beat 3 · REFRAME (8–25s)  Surprising-but-simple cause. Shifts understanding.
Beat 4 · SOLUTION (25–38s) One clear fix, feature, or next step. Specific.
Beat 5 · CLOSE (38–45s)   Relief, hope, or urgency. One to two sentences max.
```

**Master template:**
> "If you have [pain point], you may be dealing with [simple cause].
> That's why [visual/sensory symptom].
> The fix is [one clear action]."

This is the backbone. Variations are welcome; this structure is the minimum.

---

## Beat-by-Beat Writing Rules

### Beat 1 — Hook (0–3s)
- Must trigger recognition within the first two seconds.
- Names a specific symptom — not a broad topic.
  - Strong: "If you're starving one week and barely hungry the next — listen."
  - Weak: "Today we're talking about hunger and hormones."
- The viewer should think: *that's exactly what's been happening to me.*
- Avoid broad question hooks. Precision beats volume.

### Beat 2 — Sensory Detail (3–8s)
- One sentence. One physical detail. Make it bodily.
- Should describe something the viewer can feel in their own body right now.
  - Examples: tight, heavy, bloated, foggy, crashing, stuck, wired, flat, inflamed
  - Example: "That 3pm wall where your energy just drops off a cliff."
- This detail is what makes people screenshot and share.

### Beat 3 — Reframe (8–25s)
- The most important beat. This is where trust is built.
- Offers a cause that is simpler than expected, surprising but immediately
  believable, and cycle-physiology based for Lyna content.
- Commit to the explanation — no hedging, no "some people might..."
  - Strong: "That's your luteal phase shifting your blood sugar response.
    Your body is asking for different fuel than it needed last week."
  - Weak: "There could be several possible explanations for this."
- The viewer should feel: *I didn't know that. But now it makes sense.*
- For Lyna: this is where cycle phase, hormones, or nutritional patterns land.

### Beat 4 — Solution (25–38s)
- One action only. One doorway in.
- Achievable and specific. References Lyna naturally as the tool, not the subject.
  - Strong: "Scan what you're eating with Lyna. It shows you how well that
    meal matches where you are in your cycle right now."
  - Weak: "There are several things you could try to feel better."
- Lyna should appear as part of the viewer's existing life — not as a pitch.

### Beat 5 — Close (38–45s)
- Choose one of three tones based on the subject:
  - **Relief:** "Your body wasn't working against you. It just needed the right data."
  - **Hope:** "Try it for a week. Notice what shifts."
  - **Urgency:** "Download Lyna free — link in bio. Your next meal is a good place to start."
- One or two sentences maximum. The close is not for more information.

---

## Tone Directives

### Sound like:
- A calm, knowledgeable friend who understands hormones and actually wants to help
- Someone who has seen this pattern a thousand times — no drama, just clarity
- Quiet authority — not a lecturer, not a hype creator

### Never sound like:
- A brand ("LYNA helps women unlock their potential!")
- A fear marketer ("This is DESTROYING your hormones!")
- An AI assistant ("Certainly! Here are five key insights for you today.")
- A generic wellness creator (moon metaphors, lotus energy, vague positivity)

### Language rules:
- Short sentences. Spoken cadence. Max 20 words per line.
- Contractions always: "you're", "it's", "that's", "you've", "I've"
- Active voice throughout
- No filler: "it's important to note", "many people don't realise", "in conclusion"
- No banned words: game changer, life changing, obsessed, holy grail, revolutionary,
  amazing, incredible, groundbreaking

## Output Format

When asked to generate a script, produce **three versions**:

```
VERSION A — Direct and punchy (30s / ~80 words)
No extra words. Hit each beat with precision.
Tone: confident, calm, decisive.

VERSION B — Emotional and relatable (38s / ~100 words)
More feeling. The viewer feels personally understood before they feel informed.
Tone: warm, validating, slightly urgent.

VERSION C — Educational and authoritative (45s / ~120 words)
Slightly more depth on the reframe beat. More data and explanation.
Tone: measured, credible, trustworthy.
```

Format as spoken dialogue. No bullet points inside the script body itself.
Label each clip clearly if the user needs a clip breakdown for production.

## Swipe File Usage Rules

**Mix and match within category:** Any hook + any middle + any close from the same
audience category produces a complete, coherent script.

**Cross-pollinate carefully:** PCOS + Athletes overlaps well (blood sugar, energy,
performance). Endo + Perimenopause overlaps on fatigue and inflammation. Test
cross-category combinations only after you have baseline data on in-category scripts.

**Batch filming:** Keep the same setup and environment. Swap only the hook line
between takes. Film 5–8 scripts in one session — the body, middle, and close can
stay nearly identical for variants within the same audience.

**CTA rotation:** Test soft ("Try Lyna free — link in bio") vs. directional ("Download
Lyna and scan your next meal") vs. social proof ("Join 15,000+ women using Lyna").
Run each for 48 hours minimum before concluding.
