# Target Format

**Duration:** 45–90 seconds of spoken content (approximately 120–220 words).
**Platform:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts.
**Style:** Talking head, direct to camera, UGC-adjacent.

This is not a brand explainer. It is not an ad. It is a short education piece that
happens to end with a CTA. The content earns the CTA.

The 45–90s window gives room for one additional beat of depth — a second example,
a stronger proof moment, or a more developed reframe. Use that room deliberately.
Every extra sentence must earn its place. Filler still kills retention.

For app or tracking-specific content, the CTA naturally references cycle-aware tracking as a helpful tool — never as the subject of the video.

---

# What Makes This Style Work

Three psychological mechanics drive retention and conversion:

### 1. Immediate identification

The opening line makes the viewer think:

> "That's exactly what's been happening to me."

It names a specific symptom or frustration — not a broad topic.

### 2. Sensory anchoring

One concrete physical detail makes the problem feel real and remembered.

This is the detail that triggers pause and share behaviour.

### 3. Reframe + relief

A simple, surprising cause explanation makes the viewer feel smart for finally
understanding their body.

The solution is the payoff.
The close seals it with relief, hope, or urgency.

At 45–90s, the reframe beat has room to breathe. Use it to add one layer of
depth — a second consequence, a "here's why that happens" follow-on, or a
brief before/after contrast. Do not use the extra time for more product features.

Pacing is fast but never rushed. Every sentence earns its place.

# Script Structure

## Core: 5 Beats (45–60s)

Use this as your default for scripts landing in the 45–60s range.

```text
Beat 1 · HOOK (0–4s)
Sharp symptom or frustration. One sentence. Personal.

Beat 2 · SENSORY (4–10s)
One visual or bodily detail that makes it feel real.

Beat 3 · REFRAME (10–35s)
Surprising-but-simple cause. Shifts understanding.
At this length, the reframe can include one follow-on "here's why" layer.

Beat 4 · SOLUTION (35–52s)
One clear fix, feature, or next step. Specific.

Beat 5 · CLOSE (52–60s)
Relief, hope, or urgency. One to two sentences max.

```

---

## Extended: 6 Beats (60–90s)

Use this when the topic needs a proof moment, a contrast, or a second example
to land properly. The sixth beat sits between the reframe and the solution.

```text
Beat 1 · HOOK (0–4s)
Sharp symptom or frustration. One sentence. Personal.

Beat 2 · SENSORY (4–10s)
One visual or bodily detail that makes it feel real.

Beat 3 · REFRAME (10–30s)
Surprising-but-simple cause. Keep this tight — the proof beat follows.

Beat 4 · PROOF (30–52s)
A second layer: a contrast, a before/after, a "and here's what that means
for you" moment, or a supporting data point. One concrete example only.

Beat 5 · SOLUTION (52–72s)
One clear fix, feature, or next step. Specific.

Beat 6 · CLOSE (72–90s)
Relief, hope, or urgency. One to two sentences max.

```

**When to use the 6-beat structure:**

* The reframe alone won't land without a concrete example
* The audience needs a before/after contrast to believe the solution
* A supporting statistic or observation adds credibility without slowing pace
* The persona is sceptical (athletes, data-driven users, PCOS audience who
have tried many things already)

**When to stay with 5 beats:**

* The hook is strong enough to carry straight to the reframe
* The topic is emotionally immediate (endo pain, TTC frustration)
* The viewer already believes the problem — they just need the solution

---

### Master Template (5-beat)

> "If you have [pain point], you may be dealing with [simple cause].
> That's why [visual/sensory symptom].
> The fix is [one clear action]."

### Master Template (6-beat)

> "If you have [pain point], you may be dealing with [simple cause].
> That's why [visual/sensory symptom].
> And what makes it worse is [proof/contrast layer].

Variations are welcome. The structure is not optional.

# Beat-by-Beat Writing Rules

## Beat 1 — Hook (0–4s)

* Must trigger recognition within the first two seconds
* Names a specific symptom — not a broad topic
* The viewer should immediately think:
> "That's me."



### Strong

> "If you're starving one week and barely hungry the next — listen."

### Weak

> "Today we're talking about hunger and hormones."

Avoid broad question hooks. Precision beats volume.

---

## Beat 2 — Sensory Detail (4–10s)

One sentence. One physical detail. Make it bodily.

### Examples

* tight
* heavy
* bloated
* foggy
* crashing
* stuck
* wired but tired
* flat
* inflamed

### Example

> "That 3 PM wall where your energy just drops off a cliff."

This detail drives screenshots, shares, and retention.

---

## Beat 3 — Reframe (10–35s in 5-beat / 10–30s in 6-beat)

The most important beat.

This is where trust is built.

The explanation should feel:

* surprising
* simple
* believable
* immediately useful

No hedging.

### Strong

> "That's your luteal phase shifting your blood sugar response.
> Your body is asking for different fuel than it needed last week."

### Weak

> "There could be several possible explanations for this."

The viewer should feel:

> "I didn't know that. But now it makes sense."

**At 45–90s, the reframe can include one follow-on layer:**

> "That's your luteal phase shifting your blood sugar response.
> Your body is asking for different fuel than it needed last week.
> And when it doesn't get it, it escalates — which is why the cravings
> feel impossible to ignore, not just uncomfortable."

Do not stack multiple mechanisms. One cause, one consequence maximum.

For:

* TCM content → use energetic or pattern-based reframes
* Cycle-aware content → use cycle physiology and nutrition reframes
* Functional wellness → blend physiology with accessible language

---

## Beat 4 — Proof (30–52s, 6-beat only)

A grounding layer between the reframe and the solution.

Use one of:

* **Before/after contrast:** "Most women track their food the same way every
day of their cycle. Once they start adjusting by phase, the cravings change."
* **Supporting observation:** "This is why you might notice your hunger patterns
shift by day 18 even when nothing else in your week has changed."
* **Consequence stack:** "Without the right fuel in that phase, the cravings
escalate, the energy crashes, and the workout two days later suffers too."

Rules:

* One example only. No lists.
* Concrete and specific. Not abstract.
* Should make the solution feel inevitable — not like a pitch.

---

## Beat 5 — Solution (35–52s in 5-beat / 52–72s in 6-beat)

One action only. One doorway in.

The solution must feel:

* achievable
* practical
* immediate
* low friction

### Strong

> "Scan what you're eating with a cycle-aware tracker. It shows you how well that meal matches where you are in your cycle right now."

### Weak

> "There are several things you could try."

### TCM Solutions

* warming practices
* pressure points
* food adjustments
* breathing
* circulation support

### Tracking Solutions

App/tracking solutions appear as part of the viewer's existing life — not as a pitch.

Core positioning:

> track it → see it → understand it → respond better

---

## Beat 6 — Close (52–60s in 5-beat / 72–90s in 6-beat)

All CTAs must follow Agent 02’s CTA Writing Rules for the chosen video type. Do not use direct 'Download [App]' CTAs unless explicitly requested. Choose your CTA based on the video type:

### Type 1 (Pure Educational)

Engagement or follow-based CTA. No product.

> "Save this — you'll want to come back to it."
> "Follow if this is the kind of thing you've been trying to understand."

### Type 2 (Soft Funnel)

Soft bio link pointer to a quiz or resource. One sentence. Curiosity-led.

> "If you want to understand your symptoms better, there’s a quick quiz in my bio..."
> "Everyone's hormone picture is a bit different — the quiz in my bio figures out yours."

### Type 3 (Ancestral / TCM)

Lineage / Heritage Follow. Broad, trust-building invitation.

> "Follow if you want to learn how women have supported these patterns naturally for generations..."
> "If you want to reconnect with how our ancestors handled these shifts naturally, stay along with me."
