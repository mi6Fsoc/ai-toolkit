# Target Format

**Duration:** 30–45 seconds of spoken content (approximately 80–120 words).
**Platform:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts.
**Style:** Talking head, direct to camera, UGC-adjacent.

This is not a brand explainer. It is not an ad. It is a short education piece that
happens to end with a CTA. The content earns the CTA.

For Lyna-specific content, the CTA naturally references the app as a helpful tool —
never as the subject of the video.

---

# What Makes This Style Work

Three psychological mechanics drive retention and conversion:

### 1. Immediate identification

The opening line makes the viewer think:

> "That's exactly what's been happening to me."

It names a specific symptom or frustration — not a broad topic.

### 2. Sensory anchoring

One concrete physical detail makes the problem feel real and remembered.

This is the detail that triggers pause and share behaviour.

### 3. Reframe + relief

A simple, surprising cause explanation makes the viewer feel smart for finally
understanding their body.

The solution is the payoff.
The close seals it with relief, hope, or urgency.

Pacing is fast but never rushed. Every sentence earns its place.

# Script Structure (5 Beats — Non-Negotiable)

```text
Beat 1 · HOOK (0–3s)
Sharp symptom or frustration. One sentence. Personal.

Beat 2 · SENSORY (3–8s)
One visual or bodily detail that makes it feel real.

Beat 3 · REFRAME (8–25s)
Surprising-but-simple cause. Shifts understanding.

Beat 4 · SOLUTION (25–38s)
One clear fix, feature, or next step. Specific.

Beat 5 · CLOSE (38–45s)
Relief, hope, or urgency. One to two sentences max.
```

### Master Template

> "If you have [pain point], you may be dealing with [simple cause].
> That's why [visual/sensory symptom].
> The fix is [one clear action]."

Variations are welcome. The structure is not optional.

---

# Beat-by-Beat Writing Rules

## Beat 1 — Hook (0–3s)

* Must trigger recognition within the first two seconds
* Names a specific symptom — not a broad topic
* The viewer should immediately think:

  > "That's me."

### Strong

> "If you're starving one week and barely hungry the next — listen."

### Weak

> "Today we're talking about hunger and hormones."

Avoid broad question hooks. Precision beats volume.

---

## Beat 2 — Sensory Detail (3–8s)

One sentence. One physical detail. Make it bodily.

### Examples

* tight
* heavy
* bloated
* foggy
* crashing
* stuck
* wired but tired
* flat
* inflamed

### Example

> "That 3 PM wall where your energy just drops off a cliff."

This detail drives screenshots, shares, and retention.

---

## Beat 3 — Reframe (8–25s)

The most important beat.

This is where trust is built.

The explanation should feel:

* surprising
* simple
* believable
* immediately useful

No hedging.

### Strong

> "That's your luteal phase shifting your blood sugar response.
> Your body is asking for different fuel than it needed last week."

### Weak

> "There could be several possible explanations for this."

The viewer should feel:

> "I didn't know that. But now it makes sense."

For:

* TCM content → use energetic or pattern-based reframes
* Lyna content → use cycle physiology and nutrition reframes
* Functional wellness → blend physiology with accessible language

---

## Beat 4 — Solution (25–38s)

One action only. One doorway in.

The solution must feel:

* achievable
* practical
* immediate
* low friction

### Strong

> "Scan what you're eating with Lyna. It shows you how well that meal matches where you are in your cycle right now."

### Weak

> "There are several things you could try."

### TCM Solutions

* warming practices
* pressure points
* food adjustments
* breathing
* circulation support

### Lyna Solutions

Lyna appears as part of the viewer's existing life — not as a pitch.

Core positioning:

> track it → see it → understand it → respond better

---

## Beat 5 — Close (38–45s)

Choose one emotional lane:

### Relief

> "Your body wasn't working against you. It just needed the right data."

### Hope

> "Try it for a week. Notice what shifts."

### Urgency

> "Download free — link in bio. Your next meal is a good place to start."

Maximum two sentences.

## Never sound like:

* A brand
* A hype creator
* A fear marketer

# Language Rules

* Short sentences
* Spoken cadence
* Max 20 words per line
* Contractions always:

  * you're
  * it's
  * that's
  * you've
  * I've
* Active voice throughout
* No filler phrases

### Avoid:

* "it's important to note"
* "many people don't realise"
* "in conclusion"

### Banned Words

* game changer
* life changing
* obsessed
* holy grail
* revolutionary
* amazing
* incredible
* groundbreaking
