## PHASE 1 — SCRIPTING

## Video Type Arcs

### Types 1 & 2 (shared arc):

- HOOK — Stop the scroll, immediate identification. One bold claim or recognition statement. (See Viral Script Framework below.)

- SUSPENSE TRANSITION — Build anticipation before delivering value. Bridge the hook to the explanation with a line that deepens curiosity. (e.g., "The first thing you must understand is…")

- EXPLANATION — Clear WHY, not clinical. Explain the biological mechanism behind the problem in warm, accessible language. Paint the picture of what's happening inside the body.

- ATTENTION RE-GRAB — Mid-script pattern interrupt. Reset viewer attention with a suspense line before delivering the solution. (e.g., "But here is what most people do not know.")

- SOLUTION + INSIGHT — Name the specific remedy, nutrient, or practice. Explain WHY it works — the mechanism, not just the claim. Connect the solution back to the problem explained earlier.

- DIRECT ACTION STEPS — Concrete, immediately actionable guidance. Specific foods, quantities, timing, sourcing details. Leave nothing vague.

- HOPE — What this means going forward

- CTA — Type 1: engagement/follow. Type 2: soft bio link quiz pointer.

## Viral Script Framework

Every script follows a 7-beat framework designed to maximize retention, emotional engagement, and shareability. The framework governs both **structure** (how the topic is presented) and **wording** (how each line creates emotion, expectation, and suspense).

### The 7 Beats

| Beat | Purpose | Wording Principle | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. HOOK | Stop the scroll with a bold, specific claim or recognition | Certainty + specificity. No hedging. Speak as undeniable fact. | First 3 seconds |
| 2. SUSPENSE TRANSITION | Bridge hook to explanation; deepen curiosity before delivering value | Build expectation — hint that something deeper is coming. Use "The first thing you must understand…" or "Here is why this matters…" | Seconds 3–6 |
| 3. PROBLEM EXPLANATION | Explain WHY the problem exists — the biological mechanism in plain language | Paint a vivid, accessible picture of what's happening inside the body. Use concrete imagery, not clinical jargon. | Seconds 6–18 |
| 4. ATTENTION RE-GRAB | Mid-script pattern interrupt — reset viewer attention before the solution | Create a micro-suspense moment. Use "But here is what most people do not know" or "And this is where it gets interesting." | 1–2 seconds |
| 5. SOLUTION + BENEFITS | Name the specific remedy/nutrient/practice and explain WHY it works | Connect the solution directly to the problem mechanism. Show causality — not just "take X" but "X does Y because Z." | Seconds 20–35 |
| 6. DIRECT ACTION STEPS | Give concrete, immediately actionable guidance | Specific foods, quantities, timing, sourcing. No vagueness. The viewer should be able to act TODAY. | Seconds 35–45 |
| 7. CTA | Drive engagement, sharing, or follow | Warm, direct, personal. Tie back to the value delivered. | Final 3–5 seconds |

### Wording Rules (Apply to Every Beat)

**Creating Emotion:**
- Speak to the viewer's lived experience, not abstract concepts
- Use sensory and physical language ("dissolves", "rebuilds", "pushes into")
- Frame the body as alive and intelligent, not broken ("your teeth are living structures designed to constantly rebuild themselves")

**Creating Expectation:**
- Each line must make the next line feel necessary
- Use forward-pointing language: "Here is what is happening", "This is why", "The first thing you must understand"
- Never front-load the full answer — earn it through the explanation

**Creating Suspense:**
- Withhold the solution until the problem is fully painted
- Use mid-script re-grabs to reset attention before the payoff
- Transition lines should feel like a door opening, not a summary ("But here is what most people do not know" — not "Now let me explain the solution")

### Strong vs. Weak Wording Examples

| Beat | ❌ Weak (Generic/Boring) | ✅ Strong (Viral/Engaging) |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | "Cavities are a common dental problem" | "You can reverse cavities without ever going to the dentist, my friend" |
| Suspense Transition | "Let me explain how teeth work" | "The first thing you must understand is that your teeth are not just dead rocks inside your head" |
| Problem Explanation | "Sugar causes tooth decay" | "The modern diet feeds bacteria that create acid, and that acid dissolves your teeth while you sleep" |
| Attention Re-grab | "There is also a solution" | "But here is what most people do not know" |
| Solution | "Vitamin K2 is good for teeth" | "K2 takes calcium stuck in the wrong places — your arteries, your joints — and pushes it into your bones and teeth where it belongs" |
| Direct Steps | "Eat foods with K2" | "Liver, hard cheeses, egg yolks, grass-fed butter, raw sauerkraut — make sure it is raw" |
| CTA | "Like and subscribe" | "Comment 'teeth' if you found this helpful. Share this with someone who needs it" |

> **KEY PRINCIPLE:** Every line must do work. If a line doesn't create emotion, build expectation, deliver value, or create suspense — cut it. Dead air kills retention. Filler kills virality.

## Hook Rules

- Lands within 3 seconds

- Creates immediate identification/recognition

- Opens with a bold, specific claim — not a question, not a tease. State it like undeniable fact.

- Uses certainty language: "You can…", "This is why…", "Here is what…" — never "Did you know…?", "What if I told you…?", "Have you ever…?"

- No clickbait or fear manipulation

- Sounds like a trusted friend sharing something important, not a marketer selling

- The hook must be strong enough to carry the entire video if the viewer only hears the first sentence

## Tone Directives

- Sound like: Trusted, intelligent friend sharing something urgent and real. Warm, calm, never condescending. Type 3 = grounded matriarch/patriarch — earthy, unhurried, lineage-rooted.

- Emotional register: Every script must move through certainty → curiosity → understanding → empowerment. The viewer should feel progressively smarter and more capable as the script unfolds.

- Suspense pacing: Never reveal the solution before fully painting the problem. Use transition lines ("But here is what most people do not know", "And this is where it changes") to create breathing room and reset attention.

- Avoid: Corporate wellness language, mid-script disclaimers, robotic lists, hedging, fear manipulation, generic openers ("In today's video"), question hooks ("Did you know?"), AI signatures ("It's important to note", "In conclusion", "Certainly!"), filler lines that don't advance emotion or information.

- Sentence rules: Max 20 words/spoken line. One idea per sentence. Always use contractions. Active voice. Use physical/sensory verbs over abstract ones ("dissolves" not "affects", "pushes into" not "supports"). For tactile/ancestral styles, integrate transition words as physical action cues ("Here is why," "That is why").

## Hook Pattern Library

### 01 — ANATOMY REVEAL

Mechanism: Reveals hidden biological truth in warm editorial style. Curiosity through education — the body made visible, not clinical. Triggers "I didn't know that was happening inside me" recognition.

Visual Brief: Close-up/medium shot of anatomical illustration or 3D organ render on aged paper or tablet. Warm, soft rendering — not clinical/cold. Hormonal pathway diagrams (estrogen/progesterone fluctuation), hypothalamus thermostat visuals, brain-ovary axis, adrenal-cortisol overlays.

Variations:
1. 3D hypothalamus render glowing softly on aged parchment, hand-drawn annotations in faded ink
2. Cross-section of ovary with follicular phases shown as warm gradient on tablet held in avatar's hands
3. Brain-ovary axis diagram on linen-bound notebook, soft amber lamplight
4. Adrenal gland overlay with cortisol curve rendered in honey tones

Example Prompt: "Medium shot of avatar's hands resting on an aged ivory parchment displaying a warmly rendered 3D hypothalamus illustration with faded ink annotations. Soft late-morning window light from frame-left, warm 4800K. Linen counter texture visible. Vertical 9:16, iPhone-camera aesthetic. No text. No text overlay."

### 02 — BEFORE / AFTER SPLIT

Mechanism: Visual contrast IS the message. Brain processes the duality instantly — depleted vs. nourished — bypassing the need for words. Identification through recognition of the "before" state.

Visual Brief: Clean vertical split, 9:16. Left: cold, desaturated, grey-blue, depleted. Right: warm, saturated, amber, nourished. Sharp central divide, matched framing on both sides.

Variations:
1. Exhausted desaturated woman in grey-blue dawn light / same woman warm and upright in amber afternoon
2. 3am wakefulness in icy bedroom (blue cast, tense jaw) / restful sleep in warm amber bedding
3. Cortisol spike graph in cold clinical white / stable line in warm parchment tones
4. Hot flash red flush on cool skin / cooled calm skin with soft natural flush

Example Prompt: "Vertical split-screen 9:16. Left half: avatar sitting upright in bed at 3am, cool blue-grey light, desaturated skin, tense brow. Right half: same avatar peacefully asleep, warm amber lamp glow, relaxed face, honey-toned bedding. Sharp central divide. iPhone-camera aesthetic. No text. No text overlay."

### 03 — SENSORY MEDIUM SHOT WITH REMEDY

Mechanism: Tactile authority through intimate domestic ritual. The remedy is grounded in the avatar's presence — she IS the trusted source. Sensory cues (steam, texture, warmth) bypass intellectual resistance.

Visual Brief: Medium shot of avatar in frame, speaking directly to camera, holding or seated beside a warm, steaming, richly textured remedy on aged wood, stone, or linen. Steam visible as a quiet supporting element. Warm late-morning lighting. Avatar remains the anchor — the remedy never replaces her presence.

Variations:
1. Avatar cradling a magnesium-rich tart cherry nightcap in a ceramic mug
2. Avatar at a linen-covered counter with sesame-pumpkin seed bark beside her
3. Avatar holding warm bone broth in a hand-thrown earthenware bowl
4. Avatar seated beside an adaptogenic herbal tea steeping in a clear glass pot

Example Prompt: "Medium shot of avatar seated at a linen-covered counter, both hands wrapped around a ceramic mug of tart cherry nightcap, faint steam rising. Direct eye contact with camera, soft micro-lean forward. Warm late-morning window light from frame-left, ~4800K. Aged wood counter, blurred apothecary shelves behind. iPhone-camera aesthetic, vertical 9:16. No text. No text overlay."

### 04 — CURIOSITY GAP

Mechanism: The withheld element forces the brain to lean in to complete the picture. Incomplete information = compulsion to keep watching. The viewer cannot scroll until the gap closes.

Visual Brief: Something partially obscured, mid-reveal, suggestively incomplete. The withholding is intentional and clean — not chaotic.

Variations:
1. Supplement bottle label deliberately turned away from camera in avatar's hand
2. Hormone diagram on parchment with one phase blank or smudged
3. Avatar's hand pointing just off-frame to something unseen
4. Split comparison with one side intentionally blurred or covered

Example Prompt: "Medium close-up of avatar holding an amber glass supplement bottle, label deliberately rotated away from the camera, her thumb resting near the cap. Direct eye contact, calm composed expression, slight knowing brow lift. Warm window light from frame-left. Soft-focused kitchen behind. iPhone-camera aesthetic, vertical 9:16. No text. No text overlay."

### 05 — DIRECT CONFRONTATION

Mechanism: Eye contact at extreme close range collapses the distance between creator and viewer. The composed expression signals authority without aggression — "I see you, and I already know." Validation register, not pity.

Visual Brief: ECU — eyes to collarbone maximum. Expression: composed, unhurried, calm authority. The unspoken line: "I know exactly what this is."

Variations:
1. Sub-type A (38–45): eyes softened with recognition — validation register, slight orbicularis oculi engagement
2. Sub-type B (45–52): direct steady gaze, slight chin tilt forward — respected, not pitied
3. Subtle nod beginning as frame opens — acknowledgment before words
4. Stillness so complete it reads as authority — no micro-movement except blink

Example Prompt: "Extreme close-up of avatar, frame from eyes to collarbone. Direct unwavering eye contact with camera, orbicularis oculi softened in recognition, slight chin tilt forward, lips closed in calm composure. Warm window light from frame-left catching one iris. Soft-focused warm domestic background. iPhone-camera aesthetic, vertical 9:16. No text. No text overlay."

Mechanism: Cognitive dissonance forces the scroll to stop. Real domestic warmth + impossible object = the brain cannot categorise it, so it watches. Myth-busting through visual paradox.
