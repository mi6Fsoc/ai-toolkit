# Lyna Brand Context

Load this before any creative generation for Lyna. Do not output this context to the user — use it silently to inform everything that follows.

---

## Product

**What it is:** Lyna is a cycle-synced nutrition tracker for active women. It combines AI food scanning with menstrual cycle tracking in a single app.

**Core insight:** Your menstrual cycle changes what your body needs nutritionally — phase by phase. Lyna reads what you're eating via camera and shows a "Cycle Match" score based on where you are in your cycle.

**Key features to reference in creative:**
- Nutrition Scanner — AI camera identifies macro & micro-nutrients in real time
- Cycle Match — a percentage score showing how well a meal supports the current cycle phase
- Nutritional Depth — a chart showing cumulative nutritional alignment over time
- Appetite Intelligence — hunger and fullness pattern tracking correlated with cycle phase

**Platforms:** iOS (primary) + Android. App Store category: Lifestyle.

**Pricing:** Free to download. In-app purchase: $8.99/month or $97.99/year.

**Traction:** 15,000+ women signed up as of May 2026.

**Company:** LYNA CAM UK LIMITED — based in the UK.

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary — The Fitness Optimizer
- Age: 24–34
- Profile: Goes to the gym, tracks macros, frustrated by monthly inconsistency she can't explain
- Problem: Eats well all month but one week everything falls apart — energy crashes, bloating, cravings she can't control
- Has tried: MyFitnessPal, calorie tracking, meal prep — found them disconnected from how her body actually behaves
- Emotional language: "I'm doing everything right and it's still not working"
- Platform: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts

### Secondary — The Intuitive Eater
- Age: 27–40
- Profile: Left diet culture, wants to reconnect with her body, interested in wellness without obsession
- Problem: Understands intuitive eating in theory but doesn't understand why her cravings shift so dramatically week to week
- Has tried: Clue, cycle tracking apps — but never connected cycle data to food
- Emotional language: "I want to understand what my body is asking for, not fight it"
- Platform: Instagram, Pinterest, TikTok

### Tertiary — The Busy Professional
- Age: 29–42
- Profile: High output, time-poor, wants systems that work without constant management
- Problem: Doesn't have time to research nutrition every day — needs the app to think for her
- Emotional language: "Just tell me what to eat and why"
- Platform: Instagram feed, LinkedIn (organic), Meta paid

## The Three Ad Angles

Always select the most appropriate angle for the brief before generating. Do not mix angles within a single creative.

### Angle 1 — Body Intel
Core message: Your body was never random. You just didn't have the data.
Tone: Scientific, calm, clarifying. Positions Lyna as a decoder for signals the body was already sending.
Best for: Intuitive Eater persona, brand-awareness top-of-funnel, Instagram feed.

### Angle 2 — The Frustration
Core message: You eat well. One week destroys your progress. That's not failure — that's your cycle. Now you can work with it.
Tone: Validating, relatable, empowering. Meets the user in a moment of frustration and reframes it.
Best for: Fitness Optimizer persona, TikTok, Reels, high-intent scroll.

### Angle 3 — The Scan
Core message: Point your camera at food. See exactly what it means for your body today.
Tone: Product-demo, high-novelty, visual-first. Lets the product speak without emotional setup.
Best for: Busy Professional persona, Meta paid, anyone who needs to see before they believe.

## Brand Voice

**Write like this:**
- Calm and confident — never shouts, never hypes
- Data-forward — uses numbers, scores, and specific cycle phases
- Empowering but not preachy — gives women information, doesn't lecture
- Science-backed but not clinical — accessible without being dumbed down
- Honest about what the product is — never overpromises

**Sound like:** Oura Ring's tone meets a smart friend who happens to be a nutritionist.

**Do not sound like:** Flo app, Goop, any brand that uses moon metaphors, girlboss wellness, or clinical pharmaceutical copy.

## Visual Design System

### Color palette
- Primary background: Near-black #0D0D12 (Void)
- Secondary background: Deep purple-black #1A1427 (Deep)
- Hero accent: Lavender #C084FC (Lyna purple)
- Secondary accent: Rich violet #7C3AED (Cycle)
- Positive/match: Mint green #34D399 (Match)
- Alert/warning: Soft red #F87171 (Alert)
- Warm neutral: Parchment #E8D5B7 (Warm)
- Near-white: #F8F8F6 (Near)

### Never use
- Pink of any shade
- Dusty rose
- Wellness beige
- Pastel green
- Any colour associated with traditional period tracking apps (Flo pink, Clue red)

### Lighting for video
- Natural window light preferred — diffused, directional, not harsh
- Golden hour acceptable for outdoor shots
- Real kitchen environments, not studio sets
- No ring light aesthetic
- No Instagram-filter warmth

### Talent direction
- Women aged 24–40, active but not gym-obsessed in appearance
- Real skin, real environments, no model energy
- Relaxed posture, natural eye contact, natural pauses between sentences
- Not performing wellness — living it

### Typography (for UI overlays and text-on-screen moments)
- Thin sans-serif, white on dark
- Data values should be prominent: "Cycle Match: 94%" at readable size
- Cycle phase labels: "Day 18 · Luteal phase" as a secondary line below scores
- Minimal copy on screen — one insight per frame

## Signature UI Moments

These are the product's most visually distinctive moments. Include at least one in every cinematic creative.

1. **The Scan** — Camera hovering over a plate, scanning animation activates, "Cycle Match: [score]%" fades in with a soft green pulse. The score counts up from 0 to final value over 1–2 seconds.

2. **The Insight** — Phone screen showing: cycle phase indicator, Nutritional Depth chart filling left-to-right, key micronutrient flags (e.g. "Iron ↑ · Magnesium ↑").

3. **The Push** — Notification-style overlay: "Day 22 — Iron needs rise. Your meal today covered 94%."

## What the Ads Must Answer

Every Lyna creative should implicitly answer: **"Why not just use Clue + MyFitnessPal?"**

The answer is: *intelligence between the two.* Lyna doesn't just track — it connects cycle data to nutrition guidance in real time. Neither Clue nor MyFitnessPal does this. That connection is the product's entire reason to exist. Make it felt, not just stated.

## Compliance Notes

- Do not claim the app diagnoses, treats, or prevents any medical condition.
- Do not claim the food scanning is medically accurate — frame it as guidance, not measurement.
- Do not reference specific medical conditions (PCOS, endometriosis, etc.) in ad creative without legal clearance.
- The App Store description is currently more conservative than the website — align claims to the more conservative version when generating ad copy for iOS-targeted placements.
