### 3.2 Voice Attributes

Choose 3-5 core attributes that define your voice. For each, provide definition, do's, and don't's.

#### Attribute 1: [e.g., Empathetic]

**Definition:**
We listen first, acknowledge pain, and offer solutions without judgment.

**We DO:**
- ✅ Acknowledge the user's frustration before offering a solution
- ✅ Use "we understand" and "you're right to be concerned"
- ✅ Validate emotions before pivoting to logic

**We DON'T:**
- ❌ Dismiss concerns with "just" or "simply"
- ❌ Blame the user for errors
- ❌ Use corporate jargon to deflect

**Example:**
- **Good:** "We know how frustrating it is when things don't work as expected. Here's what happened and how we're fixing it."
- **Bad:** "Simply clear your cache and try again."

---

#### Attribute 2: [e.g., Authoritative]

**Definition:**
We know our stuff. We don't guess; we state facts backed by expertise.

**We DO:**
- ✅ Use data and research to support claims
- ✅ Speak with confidence, not arrogance
- ✅ Admit when we don't know something (and commit to finding out)

**We DON'T:**
- ❌ Use hedging language like "maybe," "possibly," "we think"
- ❌ Overstate capabilities or make promises we can't keep
- ❌ Use buzzwords to sound smart

**Example:**
- **Good:** "Based on our analysis of 10,000+ users, this approach increases conversion by 34%."
- **Bad:** "This might possibly help improve your results, we think."

---

#### Attribute 3: [e.g., Concise]

**Definition:**
We respect the user's time. No fluff, no filler, just what matters.

**We DO:**
- ✅ Lead with the most important information
- ✅ Use short sentences and paragraphs
- ✅ Cut unnecessary adjectives and adverbs

**We DON'T:**
- ❌ Bury the lede with long introductions
- ❌ Use three words when one will do
- ❌ Add filler like "in order to" (just say "to")

**Example:**
- **Good:** "Your account is ready. Start here."
- **Bad:** "We are pleased to inform you that your account has been successfully created and is now ready for you to begin utilizing our platform."

#### Words We Ban (The Blacklist)

These words are overused, vague, or don't align with our voice.

**Never Use:**
- ❌ **"Synergy"** — Meaningless corporate jargon
- ❌ **"Disrupt"** — Overused in tech, lacks substance

- ❌ **"Leverage"** — Use "use" instead

- ❌ **"Solution"** — Too vague; be specific about what we offer

- ❌ **"Game-changer"** — Overused and unsubstantiated
- ❌ **"Cutting-edge"** — Show, don't tell

| Synergy | Collaboration, integration, combined effect |

### 3.4 Sentence Structure & Rhythm

#### Sentence Length Guidelines
- **Short (1-10 words):** Use for impact, urgency, clarity
- **Medium (11-20 words):** Use for explanation, description
- **Long (21+ words):** Use sparingly, only when necessary for complex ideas

**Ideal Mix:** 60% medium, 30% short, 10% long

---

#### Rhythm Patterns

**Pattern 1: Short. Medium. Short.**
- Creates emphasis and memorability
- Example: "We're different. We don't just talk about innovation—we ship it. Every day."

**Pattern 2: Question. Answer.**
- Engages the reader and provides clarity
- Example: "Why does this matter? Because your time is valuable."

**Pattern 3: List of Three**
- Memorable and complete-feeling
- Example: "Fast. Reliable. Secure."

### 3.5 Content Type-Specific Tone Adjustments

Different contexts require different tone calibrations.

#### Marketing Copy
- **Tone Shift:** +2 points toward Enthusiastic
- **Why:** Need to generate excitement and action
- **Example:** "Transform your workflow in minutes, not months."

#### Product Documentation
- **Tone Shift:** +3 points toward Matter-of-fact
- **Why:** Users need clear, accurate information
- **Example:** "Click 'Settings' to configure your preferences."

#### Error Messages
- **Tone Shift:** +2 points toward Empathetic, +1 toward Casual
- **Why:** Users are frustrated; we need to be helpful and human
- **Example:** "Oops, something went wrong. We're on it. Try again in a moment."

#### Sales Conversations
- **Tone Shift:** +1 toward Formal, +2 toward Authoritative
- **Why:** Building trust and credibility
- **Example:** "Based on your needs, here's what we recommend."

#### Social Media
- **Tone Shift:** +2 toward Casual, +1 toward Funny
- **Why:** Platform expectations and engagement
- **Example:** "Monday mood: ☕️ → 💻 → 🎉"

#### Customer Support
- **Tone Shift:** +3 toward Empathetic, +1 toward Casual
- **Why:** Users need reassurance and understanding
- **Example:** "I totally get why that's frustrating. Let me help you fix it."

## 6. Usage Workflow for AI

When generating content, code comments, or strategy docs, apply the DNA as follows:

### Step 1: Context Check
**Before generating any content, ask:**
- Who is the audience? (Refer to **Section 2.1**)
- What is their current emotional state? (Refer to **Section 4.2**)
- What job are they trying to do? (Refer to **Section 2.2**)

---

### Step 2: Tone Calibration
**Compare output against Section 3.1:**
- Is it too casual or too formal?
- Is it too enthusiastic or too dry?
- Adjust based on content type (Section 3.5)

---

### Step 3: Value Alignment
**Ask: "Does this align with our Core Values (Section 1.2)?"**
- If suggesting a feature: Does it reflect our values?
- If writing copy: Does it demonstrate our values in action?
- If making a decision: Would this make our values proud or compromise them?

---

### Step 4: Vocabulary Enforcement
**Scan for banned words (Section 3.3):**
- Replace with approved alternatives
- Ensure power words are used appropriately
- Check that language matches our voice attributes

---

### Step 5: Message Hierarchy
**Ensure content follows the hierarchy (Section 5.3):**
- Lead with the primary message
- Support with proof points
- End with a clear call to action

---

### Step 6: Objection Handling
**Anticipate resistance (Section 5.4):**
- What objections might this content trigger?
- Have we preemptively addressed them?
- Is the reframe clear and compelling?
