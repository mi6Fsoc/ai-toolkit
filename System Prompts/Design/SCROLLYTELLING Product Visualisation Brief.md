# SCROLLYTELLING Product Visualisation Brief

## 1. EXPLODED VIEW END FRAME

### 1.1 Primary Objective

Generate a museum-grade, hyper-realistic exploded view that reveals the complete internal architecture, precision engineering, and fluid dynamics of **[PRODUCT NAME]** with cinematic impact suitable for premium brand storytelling.

---

### 1.2 Core Visual Concept

### Product Deconstruction Architecture

Create a meticulously engineered exploded diagram of the **[PRODUCT TYPE]** (e.g., espresso machine, hydraulic pump, smart vaporizer, precision water filtration system) where:

- **Every component** is separated along its authentic mechanical assembly axis
- **Zero to minimal rotation** of individual parts from their operational orientation
- **Hierarchical spacing** that maintains visual clarity while preserving assembly logic
- **Grouped sub-assemblies** (e.g., motor housing with stator/rotor, PCB with mounted components) remain clustered but separated from adjacent systems

### Fluid Dynamics Integration (If Applicable)

For products that contain, process, or dispense liquids:

**Fluid must be captured as a dynamic, frozen explosion** exhibiting:

- **Realistic fragmentation:** Spherical droplets (2-8mm), elongated ligaments, fine mist aerosols, and larger globules based on fluid velocity and viscosity
- **Trajectory accuracy:** Liquid suspended exactly along operational flow paths—inside transparent tubing, mid-ejection from nozzles, pooling in reservoirs, or coating internal surfaces
- **Physical behavior:** Larger droplets near low-velocity zones, fine mist at atomization points, surface tension visible on all suspended volumes
- **Mixed states:** Where applicable, show steam plumes, condensation fog, or vapor clouds interacting with liquid phases

**Do not render fluid as:**

- Solid geometric blocks
- Uniform particle clouds
- Generic splash shapes disconnected from product function

---

### 1.3 Material Fidelity & Rendering Quality

### Photorealistic Material Standards

Each material category must exhibit microscopic authenticity:

**Metals (Aluminum, Stainless Steel, Copper, Brass):**

- CNC machining marks (0.01-0.05mm tool paths)
- Anodizing gradients or passivation layers
- Edge chamfers and relief cuts
- Heat-affected zones near welds
- Slight oxidation on brass/copper

**Plastics & Polymers:**

- Injection molding gate marks and ejector pin dimples
- Subtle sink marks on thick sections
- Realistic texture (matte, semi-gloss, or high-gloss with appropriate micro-scratching)
- Color variation from pigment distribution
- Translucency depth on semi-transparent parts

**Electronic Components:**

- Visible solder joints with appropriate fillet geometry
- Silkscreen text on PCBs (component designators, brand marks)
- Gold-plated contacts and connector pins
- SMD component placement with correct orientation
- Thermal paste residue on heat sink interfaces

**Elastomers & Seals:**

- Shore hardness-appropriate surface tension
- Compression set memory on O-rings
- Fabric reinforcement visible in high-pressure hoses
- Natural latex/silicone translucency

**Glass & Ceramics:**

- Subsurface light scattering
- Slight imperfections (micro-bubbles in glass)
- Crystalline structure hints in ceramics

**Textiles & Meshes:**

- Individual thread weave patterns
- Fabric tension and draping physics
- Filter media fiber structure

---

### 1.4 Lighting & Atmosphere

### Studio Lighting Configuration

**Primary Key Light:**

- Large softbox positioned 45° above and 30° camera-right
- Color temperature: 5600K (daylight balanced)
- Broad, even illumination across all components

**Accent Rim Lights:**

- Hard light source positioned 135° behind subject
- Catches edges of machined metal parts
- Creates brilliant highlights on suspended liquid droplets (refraction stars)
- Intensity: 150-200% of key light

**Fill Lighting:**

- Large bounce cards camera-left and below
- Reduces shadow density to maintain detail visibility
- Subtle, never flat

**Backlighting (for liquids):**

- Dedicated backlight penetrating through transparent/translucent fluids
- Creates internal glow, reveals particulates
- Simulates professional beverage photography technique

**Shadow Strategy:**

- Subtle contact shadows beneath each floating component (0.5-1.5% opacity)
- Maintains spatial anchoring without muddying the composition
- Shadows fall onto background plane, not onto other components

### Atmospheric Control

- **Zero haze, fog, or volumetric atmosphere**
- **Zero motion blur** (this is a frozen moment, not a motion capture)
- **Absolute sharpness** from foreground to background
- **No depth of field** (everything in perfect focus)

---

### 1.5 Composition & Spatial Logic

### Explosion Methodology

**Axis-Based Deconstruction:**

Components separate along one of three primary axes:

1. **Assembly axis** (how the product is built)
2. **Functional axis** (direction of force/flow during operation)
3. **Visual clarity axis** (optimized for camera readability)

**Spacing Hierarchy:**

- **Primary assemblies:** 80-150mm separation
- **Secondary components:** 40-80mm separation
- **Fasteners & small parts:** 20-40mm separation
- **Clustered sub-assemblies:** 10-25mm internal spacing

**Grouping Logic:**

Maintain engineering relationships:

- Screws hover directly above/below their threaded holes
- Gaskets positioned between the components they seal
- Wiring harnesses remain connected to both termination points (stretched, not severed)
- Bearing races separated but concentrically aligned
- Layered components (filters, screens) maintain stacking order

### Camera & Framing

- **Lens:** 85-135mm equivalent (minimal perspective distortion)
- **Angle:** Slightly elevated (10-20° above horizon) for natural 3/4 view
- **Framing:** Product silhouette occupies 70-85% of frame height
- **Headroom:** Balanced negative space above and below
- **Instant recognition:** Despite deconstruction, the overall form must immediately read as [PRODUCT NAME]

---

### 1.6 Background Specifications

**Solid Color Requirements:**

- **Pure, unblemished solid color:** [SPECIFY: Deep Onyx #0A0A0A, Charcoal #1C1C1C, Slate Grey #2D2D2D, Pure White #FFFFFF]
- **Zero gradients**
- **Zero texture or noise**
- **Zero environmental reflection** (product is lit, not reflecting an environment)
- **Engineered for clean alpha channel extraction**
- **Color tolerance:** ±1 RGB value maximum deviation across entire background

---

### 1.7 Technical Constraints & Rules

### Physical Accuracy Requirements

- **Dimensional integrity:** All components maintain exact real-world proportions
- **No interpenetration:** Parts cannot clip through each other
- **Gravity-neutral logic:** Components appear suspended by invisible forces, not defying physics awkwardly
- **Assembly coherence:** An engineer should be able to mentally reassemble the product from this image

### Forbidden Elements

- **No graphic overlays:** No labels, callouts, arrows, dimension lines, or part numbers
- **No UI elements:** No frames, borders, or interface components
- **No text:** Except authentic product text (logos, legally required markings) visible on physical components
- **No extraneous objects:** No tools, hands, surfaces, or contextual props

### Material Authenticity

Every material must be instantly identifiable:

- Rubber looks like rubber (not plastic painted black)
- Copper has actual copper coloration (not orange-tinted chrome)
- Anodized aluminum shows appropriate surface characteristics
- PCB substrate is FR-4 green/black/blue, not generic plastic

## 2. CINEMATIC TRANSITION SEQUENCE (GOOGLE FLOW)

### 2.1 Sequence Overview

**Project Identifier:** [PROJECT NAME] – Assembled to Exploded Transition

**Duration Target:** 4-8 seconds of smooth, premium motion

**Core Concept:** An elegant, engineered transformation that reveals the product's internal truth through a continuous, choreographed deconstruction—beginning with the pristine hero product and culminating in the full exploded diagram detailed in Section 1.

---

### 2.2 Opening Frame (0-15%)

### Initial Product State

**Product Presentation:**

- **[PRODUCT NAME]** fully assembled, pristine condition
- **Rotation state:** Mid-rotation (product is already in gentle motion, showing a dynamic 3/4 view)
- **Rotational speed:** 8-15° per second (slow, deliberate, premium pace)
- **Position:** Perfectly centered in frame, suspended in space
- **Lighting:** Full studio lighting (as specified in Section 1.4)

**Background & Atmosphere:**

- **Pure solid background:** [COLOR: e.g., #000000 black]
- **Zero environment**
- **Product appears to float** in infinite space
- **Calm, controlled, luxurious aesthetic**

---

### 2.3 Initial Transformation Phase (15-40%)

### Element Introduction

**[PRIMARY ELEMENTS]** begin entering frame:

*Elements defined by product type:*

- **Food/Beverage products:** Ingredients (coffee beans, water droplets, herbs, fruit)
- **Technical devices:** Raw materials (metal stock, molded components, batteries)
- **Fluid systems:** Liquid volumes, vapor clouds
- **Layered products:** Individual layers/strata of product construction

**Entry Choreography:**

- **Origin point:** Elements emerge from behind and around the product
- **Trajectory:** Follow curved paths that spiral or arc toward their operational positions
- **Speed variation:** Different element types travel at different velocities (creates depth)
- **Starting density:** Sparse (15-20 visible elements)
- **No collisions:** Elements never contact product or each other
- **Purposeful movement:** Each element follows an engineered path, not random floating

**Visual Characteristics:**

- **Ultra-sharp definition** (zero motion blur despite movement)
- **Perfect lighting** (every element receives proper key, fill, and rim lighting)
- **Readable silhouettes** at all times
- **Material fidelity** maintained throughout motion

---

### 2.4 Momentum Build Phase (40-65%)

### Escalating Deconstruction

**Element Density Increase:**

- **Exponential growth** in number of visible elements
- **Current count:** 100-250+ individual pieces/particles/components
- **Spatial distribution:** Elements now occupy full 360° around product
- **Movement becomes more complex** but remains controlled

**Product Status:**

- **Continues rotation** at consistent speed
- **Begins subtle expansion:** First hints that product will deconstruct
- **Outermost components** start to separate (0.5-2mm gaps appear)
- **Product remains fully recognizable**

**Choreographic Complexity:**

- **Layered motion:** Foreground, midground, and background elements move at different velocities
- **Spiral patterns:** Some elements trace helical paths around the product
- **Centrifugal suggestion:** Movement implies outward force originating from product core
- **Maintains premium aesthetic:** Never feels chaotic or explosive in a destructive sense

---

### 2.5 Energy Event (50-55%)

### Transformational Moment

**[ENERGY EFFECT]** manifests behind/within the product:

*Effect type defined by product character:*

- **Precision devices:** Concentric geometric wave (technical, controlled)
- **Fluid products:** Liquid arc or hydraulic surge
- **Compressed systems:** Air displacement shockwave with refraction
- **Electronic products:** Light pulse or electromagnetic field visualization
- **Impact-based products:** Clean shockwave ring

**Effect Characteristics:**

- **Origin:** Emanates from product's functional core (motor, heating element, pressure chamber)
- **Behavior:** Expands outward, partially wrapping around product circumference (180-220° wrap)
- **Opacity:** Semi-transparent (60-75% opacity at peak)
- **Interaction:** Influences nearby element trajectories without obscuring product
- **Duration:** Visible for 0.5-1.2 seconds
- **Purpose:** Visual punctuation that signals the transformation from assembled to exploded state

**Visual Restraint:**

- **Not explosive or violent**
- **Not magical or fantastical** (no sparkles, glows, or mystical effects)
- **Feels engineered and intentional**
- **Reinforces premium brand perception**

---

### 2.6 Full Deconstruction Phase (65-90%)

### Controlled Disassembly

**Product Transformation:**

- **All components separate** along their designated assembly axes
- **Separation accelerates** smoothly (easing function: ease-out)
- **Final positions match** the exploded diagram specified in Section 1
- **Rotation continues** or gradually slows to final presentation angle

**Element Behavior:**

- **[PRIMARY ELEMENTS]** reach their final suspended positions
- **Complex choreography peaks:** Maximum visual density and motion complexity
- **All elements remain sharp** and well-lit
- **Depth staging:** Clear foreground/midground/background separation

**Fluid Dynamics (if applicable):**

- **Liquid volumes fragment** into realistic droplet distributions
- **Follows actual physics:** Surface tension, viscosity-appropriate behavior
- **Positions match operational flow paths**
- **Backlit droplets sparkle** as they reach final positions

**Camera:**

- **May execute subtle camera move:** Slight push-in or orbit to enhance drama
- **Or remains locked:** If product scale/rotation provides sufficient motion

---

### 2.7 Final Frame (90-100%)

### Resolved Composition

**Product State:**

- **Fully exploded** to match Section 1 specifications exactly
- **All motion ceases** (or slows to imperceptible drift)
- **Every component perfectly positioned** in its designated location
- **Instant readability** of all internal systems

**Elements & Environment:**

- **[PRIMARY ELEMENTS] suspended** in final positions
- **All surfaces sharply focused**
- **Lighting at full intensity**
- **Background remains pure solid color**

**Compositional Polish:**

- **Balanced negative space**
- **Clear visual hierarchy** (eye naturally travels to key components)
- **Product silhouette immediately recognizable** despite deconstruction
- **Frame holds** for 1-2 seconds to allow viewer comprehension

---

### 2.8 Motion Design Principles

### Animation Quality Standards

**Easing & Timing:**

- **No linear motion:** All movement uses appropriate easing curves
- **Ease-in-out for organic elements:** Ingredients, fluids, soft materials
- **Ease-out for mechanical components:** Precise, engineered deceleration
- **Synchronized beats:** Key moments align with musical/rhythmic timing (if audio present)

**Motion Continuity:**

- **Perfectly smooth playback** at 60fps minimum (120fps preferred for slow-motion capability)
- **No stuttering or frame drops**
- **Consistent motion blur settings** (if any blur is applied, it must be high-quality per-object motion blur, though zero blur is preferred per Section 1.4)

**Spatial Awareness:**

- **Z-depth sorting maintained** throughout (objects never incorrectly overlap)
- **Camera-aware choreography:** Elements move in 3D space, not just screen space
- **Perspective-correct scaling** as objects move through depth

### Aesthetic Consistency

**Throughout entire sequence:**

- **Product always well-lit** (never falls into shadow)
- **Product always centered** (or follows intentional compositional offset)
- **Product never obscured** by passing elements
- **Shape, color, and proportions preserved** (no distortion)
- **Clean, premium appearance** maintained frame-to-frame
- **Brand values communicated:** Precision, quality, sophistication, transparency

**Background Discipline:**

- **Pure solid [BACKGROUND COLOR]** held constant
- **No gradients introduced** during motion
- **No textures or patterns**
- **No environmental effects** (lens flares, god rays, atmospheric haze)
- **Color never shifts** throughout sequence

---

### 2.9 Technical Specifications

### Rendering Requirements

- **Resolution:** 4K minimum (3840×2160), 6K or 8K preferred for premium applications
- **Frame rate:** 60fps minimum, 120fps preferred
- **Color space:** Rec. 2020 or DCI-P3
- **Bit depth:** 10-bit minimum, 16-bit preferred
- **Alpha channel:** Clean alpha matte for compositing flexibility
- **File format:** ProRes 4444 XQ, EXR sequence, or equivalent high-quality codec
