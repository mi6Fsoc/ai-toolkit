# Hyper-Realistic Image Generation Framework

## Core Structure Template

[TECHNICAL FOUNDATION] + [SUBJECT DESCRIPTION] + [COMPOSITION & FRAMING] + [LIGHTING & ATMOSPHERE] + [TEXTURE & DETAIL SPECIFICATIONS] + [STYLE & QUALITY MODIFIERS]

Negative Prompt: [QUALITY ISSUES] + [TECHNICAL ARTIFACTS] + [STYLE CONFLICTS] + [COMPOSITION PROBLEMS]

## 1. Technical Foundation

Purpose: Establishes the baseline quality and realism standards Format: Place at the very beginning of your prompt

### Essential Elements:

- Resolution/Quality: "8K ultra-high resolution, hyper-realistic, photorealistic"
- Camera Simulation: "Shot with [specific camera model], [lens type], [focal length]"
- Professional Standard: "Professional photography, award-winning photograph"

### Example Foundation:

8K ultra-high resolution, hyper-realistic photorealistic image shot with Canon EOS R5, 85mm lens, professional studio photography, award-winning commercial photograph

## 2. Subject Description

Purpose: Defines what you're photographing with precise detail Strategy: Work from general to specific, like focusing a camera lens

### Structure:

- Primary Subject: The main focus (person, object, scene)
- Physical Characteristics: Size, shape, color, texture
- State/Condition: New vs worn, active vs static, emotional state
- Context: What the subject is doing or how it's positioned

### Example:

A master craftsman's weathered leather work gloves, rich cognac brown color with natural grain patterns, slightly worn fingertips showing use, resting on an antique wooden workbench

## 3. Composition & Framing

Purpose: Controls how the image is structured and what the viewer sees Key Principle: Be specific about viewpoint and framing to avoid random compositions

### Essential Specifications:

- Shot Type: Close-up, medium shot, wide shot, macro
- Angle: Eye level, low angle, high angle, bird's eye
- Perspective: Straight-on, three-quarter view, profile
- Depth: Shallow depth of field, everything in focus, background blur

### Example:

Extreme macro close-up shot, three-quarter angle view, shallow depth of field with creamy bokeh background, sharp focus on the main subject

## 4. Lighting & Atmosphere

Purpose: Creates mood and enhances realism through believable light behavior Critical Understanding: Light makes or breaks photorealism

### Lighting Specifications:

- Light Source: Natural sunlight, studio lighting, candlelight, etc.
- Direction: Front-lit, side-lit, backlit, rim lighting
- Quality: Soft, hard, diffused, direct
- Color Temperature: Warm (golden hour), cool (overcast), neutral
- Shadows: Where they fall, how soft or hard they are

### Example:

Soft natural window light from the left side, warm golden hour color temperature, gentle shadows with smooth gradation, subtle rim lighting highlighting edges

## 5. Texture & Detail Specifications

Purpose: Achieves the "hyper-realistic" quality through surface detail Strategy: Focus on what makes materials look and feel real

### Detail Categories:

- Surface Textures: Rough, smooth, glossy, matte, fabric weave, wood grain
- Wear Patterns: Scratches, patina, fading, smoothing from use
- Reflective Properties: How light bounces off different materials
- Fine Details: Pores, fiber, crystalline structure, imperfections

### Example:

Rich leather texture with visible grain patterns, subtle scratches and wear marks, natural oils creating slight sheen, individual stitching threads clearly defined, authentic aging and patina

## 6. Style & Quality Modifiers

Purpose: Reinforces the realism and prevents AI from defaulting to stylized looks Placement: Usually at the end to reinforce everything else

### Key Modifiers:

- Realism Reinforcement: "Photojournalistic style", "documentary photography"
- Technical Excellence: "Tack sharp focus", "perfect exposure"
- Commercial Quality: "Product photography", "high-end commercial work"

### Example:

Documentary photography style, tack sharp focus, perfect exposure and color grading, commercial product photography quality

## Negative Prompt Framework

### 1. Quality Issues

blurry, soft focus, out of focus, low resolution, pixelated, compressed, artifacts, noise, grain (unless desired)

### 2. Technical Artifacts

oversaturated, overexposed, underexposed, harsh shadows, artificial lighting, digital artifacts, HDR artifacts, chromatic aberration

### 3. Style Conflicts

cartoon, illustration, painting, sketch, anime, stylized, abstract, surreal, fantastical, unrealistic colors

### 4. Composition Problems

cluttered background, distracting elements, poor framing, tilted horizon, awkward cropping, busy composition

## Pro Tips for Maximum Quality

1. Layer Your Details: Start broad, then get increasingly specific
2. Use Photography Language: Terms like "bokeh", "depth of field", and "color temperature" trigger better photographic simulation
3. Specify Materials: AI needs to know how different materials should look and behave with light
4. Control the Negative: Your negative prompt is equally important - it prevents AI from making common mistakes
5. Be Consistent: Make sure all elements support the same vision and style
